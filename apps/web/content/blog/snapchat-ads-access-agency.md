# How to Give Snapchat Ads Access to an Agency (2026)

Snapchat is no longer just an experimental channel. With over **850 million monthly active users** and a highly engaged Gen Z demographic, agencies are increasingly managing substantial ad budgets on the platform. Yet, the onboarding process often stalls on a single friction point: **access**.

Sharing login credentials is a security nightmare and violates Snapchat's terms of service. Agencies need a secure, professional way to manage client accounts without assuming ownership liability.

Here is the definitive guide to granting agency access to Snapchat Ads Manager correctly.

## Why Snapchat Access Matters for Agencies

Unlike Meta or Google, Snapchat's ad ecosystem is more contained. This creates unique challenges and opportunities.

*   **Unified Ad Accounts:** Snapchat Ads generally function as one unified account per organization, unlike the complex Business Manager hierarchies found elsewhere.
*   **Granular Control:** You can assign specific roles (Admin, Standard, Analyst) to ensure teams only see what they need.
*   **Security Compliance:** Granting access via Snapchat's official "Organizations" feature creates an audit trail, protecting both the agency and the client.

## Understanding Snapchat Ad Account Roles

Before sending an access request, identify which role your agency needs. Assigning the lowest necessary permission level is a security best practice.

| Role | Permissions | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Admin** | Full control, payment method management, user management | Agency Owners, Finance Leads, Primary Account Managers |
| **Standard** | Create/edit campaigns, view reports, manage ads | Media Buyers, Campaign Managers, Creative Teams |
| **Analyst** | View-only access for reporting and analytics | Data Analysts, Reporting Specialists, Interns |

**Pro Tip:** Always start with **Standard** access. Only elevate to Admin if the agency needs to manage payment methods or add other users.

## Method 1: The Manual Process (Agency-Side)

The most secure way to gain access is for the agency to initiate the request via their own Ads Manager. This prompts the client to approve the partnership.

### Prerequisites
*   The agency must have an active Snapchat Ads account.
*   The client must know the email address associated with the agency's Snapchat Ad Manager account.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

1.  **Navigate to Ad Accounts**
    Log in to `ads.snapchat.com`. Click the **Ad Accounts** tab in the left-hand navigation menu.

2.  **Select "Members"**
    Choose the specific Ad Account you want to manage. In the account overview, find and click on the **Members** tab in the sub-header.

3.  **Add a Member**
    Click the **+ Add Member** button (usually located in the top right).

4.  **Search by Email**
    Enter the **client's email address** associated with their Snapchat Ads account.
    
    *Note: You are technically "adding" the client to your account structure to request access to theirs, or searching for their Business ID if known.*

5.  **Assign Role**
    Select the appropriate role from the dropdown (Standard or Admin). Click **Invite**.

6.  **Client Approval**
    The client will receive an email notification.
    *   They must click the link in the email.
    *   They will be redirected to Snapchat Ads Manager to **Accept** the invitation.
    *   Once accepted, the agency account will appear in their "Members" list.

**Why This Matters:** This method confirms identity via email ownership, reducing the risk of unauthorized access.

## Method 2: The Client-Side Grant (Direct Invitation)

If the client prefers to add the agency directly (often faster if the agency is already waiting), they can initiate the invite from their account.

### Client Instructions

1.  Log in to **Ads Manager**.
2.  Go to the **Ad Accounts** tab.
3.  Select the specific account to share.
4.  Navigate to **Members** > **+ Add Member**.
5.  Enter the **Agency's email address**.
6.  Set the Role to **Standard** (recommended) or **Admin**.
7.  Click **Send Invite**.

The agency will receive an email to accept the invitation.

## Common Problems & Solutions

Snapchat's interface can sometimes be glitchy regarding permissions. Here are the most frequent issues agencies face during onboarding.

| Problem | Solution | Prevention |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **"User not found" error** | Verify the exact email address used by the agency for their Snapchat Ads account. Typos are the #1 cause. | Use a copy-paste workflow for emails. |
| **No email received** | Check spam/junk folders. Ensure no firewall rules block `@snapchat.com` or `@snap.com` domains. | Whitelist Snapchat domains before starting the onboarding process. |
| **"Pending" status stuck** | The client clicked the link but didn't fully confirm. They must log in and explicitly click "Accept" in the dashboard. | Advise the client to check the "Members" tab to see active invites. |
| **Payment method hidden** | The agency was assigned "Analyst" or a restricted "Standard" role. | Request "Admin" access only if payment management is strictly required. |
| **Wrong Ad Account** | Clients often have multiple test accounts. Verify the **Account ID** (numbers in the URL) matches the contract. | Always request the specific Account ID via the intake form before sending invites. |

## Snapchat vs. Other Platforms

While similar to Meta and TikTok, Snapchat has nuances.

| Feature | Snapchat Ads | Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | TikTok Ads |
|---------|--------------|---------------------------|------------|
| **Hierarchy** | Flat (Ad Account level) | Nested (Business Manager > Ad Account) | Nested (Business Center > Ad Account) |
| **2-Factor Auth** | Optional | Highly Recommended | Mandatory for Agencies |
| **Approval Speed** | Instant | Instant | Instant |
| **Organization Tool** | Basic | Complex (Business Manager) | Moderate (Business Center) |

**Key Takeaway:** Snapchat is simpler but offers fewer granular controls. Be extra careful assigning Admin roles since there are no "Partial Asset" permissions like in Meta.

## Security Best Practices

Agencies handling Snapchat access should adhere to these security protocols to maintain client trust.

1.  **Never Share Passwords:** There is never a reason for a client to share their Snapchat login credentials. If they ask, politely direct them to the "Members" tab.
2.  **Require 2FA:** Mandate that all agency users accessing client accounts enable Two-Factor Authentication on their own Snapchat profiles.
3.  **Revoke Access Immediately:** Set a clear offboarding policy. If a client churns, remove the agency user from the client's "Members" list immediately.
4.  **Monitor Spend:** Set up automated rules or daily checks to ensure no malicious ads are launched if an Admin account is compromised.
