# How to Get Snapchat Ads Access for Clients (2026 Guide)

Snapchat is no longer just an experimental platform. With over **850 million monthly active users** and a highly engaged Gen Z demographic, agencies are increasingly tasked with managing Snapchat Ad accounts. However, the platform's business structure differs significantly from Meta or Google Ads.

Getting access often involves navigating confusing "Business Manager" hierarchies and specific ad account IDs. If you send the wrong request or the client approves it at the wrong level, you might end up with **view-only access** or no access at all.

This guide clarifies the exact permissions needed to run campaigns successfully and how to request them without the back-and-forth.

## Why Snapchat Access Structure Confuses Agencies

Snapchat uses a two-tier system: **Snapchat Business Manager** (formerly Business Hub) and **Ad Accounts**.

Agencies commonly make the mistake of requesting access to the Business Manager alone. While this lets you see the organization exists, it does **not** grant access to run ads or spend budget. You must explicitly be invited to the specific **Ad Account ID** within that manager.

Understanding this distinction upfront saves hours of "I can't see the ads" emails on Day 1.

## Permission Levels: What You Actually Need

Snapchat grants several permission tiers. For standard agency management (creating ads, managing budgets, viewing analytics), you need **Admin** or **Standard** access on the Ad Account.

| Role | Permissions | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Admin** | Full control, including payment methods and user management | Lead strategists, Agency Owners |
| **Standard** | Create/edit campaigns, view reports, manage budgets | Daily Media Buyers, Campaign Managers |
| **Analyst** | View-only access to data and reports | Interns, Reporting Specialists |
| **Member** (Business Level) | Access to the Business entity, but **no** access to specific ad accounts | ❌ **Avoid this** |

**Pro Tip:** Always ask for **Admin** access initially. It is easier to downgrade permissions later than to ask the client to re-approve you because you can't add a pixel or edit a payment setting.

## Phase 1: Gather Required Information

Before sending a request, ensure you have the correct details. Sending a request to the wrong Ad Account is the most common cause of delays.

**Information you need from the client:**
1.  **Ad Account ID:** This usually starts with "ca-" or a series of numbers (e.g., `ca-1234567890`).
2.  **Business Manager ID (Optional):** Helpful for organizational context, but secondary to the Ad Account.
3.  **Email Address:** Use a generic agency email (e.g., `media@youragency.com`) rather than a personal employee email to prevent lockout if staff leave.

**Why this matters:** Snapchat has strict limits on how many Business Managers an email can associate with. Using a generic agency email protects your operations.

## Phase 2: The Manual Client Request Process

If you are doing this manually (without an access management tool), follow this sequence to ensure the client receives exactly what they need to approve.

### Step 1: Log into Ads Manager
Navigate to [ads.snapchat.com](https://ads.snapchat.com) and log in with your agency credentials.

### Step 2: Navigate to Account Settings
*   Click the **dropdown menu** in the top left header.
*   Select **All Accounts**.
*   Click **+ Add Account**.

### Step 3: Send the Request
You will see a prompt asking for an **Ad Account ID** or **Email** associated with the account.

*   **Best Practice:** Ask the client to invite *you* by providing them with your agency email. This reduces errors.
*   **Alternative:** Enter the client's Ad Account ID and send a request via the Snapchat interface.

**Crucial Check:** Ensure you select the correct **Agency Business Manager** to invite from (if you have multiple).

## Phase 3: Client-Side Approval Actions

Once the request is sent, the client must log in to their Ads Manager to approve it. This is where 40% of requests stall.

**Instructions to copy/paste to your client:**

> 1. Log in to your [Snapchat Ads Manager](https://ads.snapchat.com).
> 2. Click the **menu icon** (top left) and select **Member Accounts** under the specific Ad Account you want us to manage.
> 3. Click **+ Add Members**.
> 4. Enter our agency email: `[your-email@agency.com]`.
> 5. Select **Admin** or **Standard** access level.
> 6. Click **Send Invites**.

### Issue: Request Pending Indefinitely
**Cause:** The client received the email but clicked it while not logged into the correct Snapchat account, or the email was filtered to spam.
**Solution:** Ask the client to check the "Members" tab directly in the Ads Manager UI rather than clicking the email link.

### Issue: "You do not have permission to view this account"
**Cause:** You were added to the **Business Manager** (Organization) but not the specific **Ad Account**.
**Solution:** The client must go into the specific Ad Account settings (not the general Business settings) and assign the role there.

### Issue: Payment Method Unavailable
**Cause:** You have Standard access but the client's payment method requires Admin verification for every transaction.
**Solution:** Request Admin access or ask the client to add a valid payment method to the ad account manually.
