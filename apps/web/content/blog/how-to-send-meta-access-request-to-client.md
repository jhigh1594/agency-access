# How to Send a Meta Ads Access Request to Clients (2026 Guide)

Getting access to a client's Facebook and Instagram ad accounts often takes longer than setting up the campaigns themselves.

You send the request. You wait. You follow up. The client claims they clicked "Approve," but you still see "Pending." Two days later, you discover they accepted you as an **Employee** instead of an **Agency Admin**, so you still can't touch the credit card.

This friction costs agencies 3-4 hours per client. That is nearly an entire workday every month lost to access administration for an agency taking on just 3-4 new clients.

Meta's Business Manager interface changes frequently, which confuses clients. If they use an outdated link or click the wrong button, the request fails silently.

Here is the precise workflow to send access requests in 2026, why the manual process breaks down, and how to collect permissions instantly.

## Why Meta Access Requests Fail

Understanding the common failure points helps you guide clients through the process.

**The "Ghost" Request:** Meta filters requests into a "Requests" folder in Business Settings. Clients often miss this notification entirely if they don't have email notifications turned on.

**The Wrong ID:** You send a request for the wrong Business ID. Agencies often have multiple Business Manager accounts. If you request access via ID "A" but the client's asset lives in ID "B," the request will arrive, but the asset won't be there to assign.

**Permission Incompatibility:** You request "Ad Account Advertiser" access, but the client tries to assign you as an "Employee" (a common mistake for non-technical clients). Meta's interface defaults to standard employee roles, which do not include financial access.

## Method 1: The Manual Request Process

Use this method when you must interact directly inside Meta Business Suite. This requires the client to perform the final approval action.

### Step 1: Identify the Correct Business ID
1. Navigate to **Business Settings** in your agency's Business Manager.
2. Click **Business Info** on the left sidebar.
3. Copy the **Business ID** from the right-hand panel. You need this to ensure the client connects to the correct agency entity.

### Step 2: Inspect the Client's Ad Account
Before sending the request, verify you have the correct Ad Account ID.
1. Ask the client for their Ad Account ID (format: `act_1234567890`).
2. Go to **Ad Accounts** in your Business Manager.
3. Click **Add** > **Add People**.
4. Paste the client's Ad Account ID.
5. If you receive an "Invalid ID" error, stop. You do not have the correct ID, or the account is disabled.

### Step 3: Send the Request
1. Go to **Business Settings** > **Users** > **People**.
2. Select your name (or the agency employee needing access).
3. Toggle to the **Ad Accounts** tab.
4. Click **Add** > **Assign Access**.
5. Select **Access to my ad accounts** (this sends a request to *them* to add you).

**Note:** This sends a notification to the client's email and their Business Manager notification center.

### Step 4: Client-Side Approval
Send these exact instructions to your client to minimize error:

1. Go to **Business Settings** > **Notifications** (bell icon).
2. Open the request from "[Your Agency Name]".
3. Select the **Ad Account** from the list.
4. Toggle the switch to **Manage Ad Campaigns** (Ad Account Advertiser).
5. **CRITICAL:** Check the box for **Campaign Budget Management** if you need to edit budgets.
6. Click **Confirm**.

### Common Manual Request Errors

| Error | Cause | Fix |
|-------|-------|-----|
| "You don't have permission" | Client assigned you as an Employee, not an Admin. | Client must remove and re-invite with "Ad Account Advertiser" permissions. |
| No notification received | Email notifications are off in Business Settings. | Client must check the "Requests" tab in Business Settings manually. |
| Pending forever | Client clicked "Ignore" accidentally. | Send the request again from your end. |

## Pro Tips for Faster Meta Onboarding

### Pro Tip: The "Two-Step" Verification
Always verify the *Business ID* matches the request. If you have access to the client's Page but not the Ad Account, check the Page Info. The Business ID listed there is likely where the Ad Account lives.

### Pro Tip: Video Walkthroughs
Send a 30-second Loom video showing exactly where the "Confirm" button is. Clients respond better to visual cues than text blocks. Mark up the screenshot with a red circle around the specific toggle.

### Pro Tip: Use a Secondary Admin
If the primary contact is the business owner (who is rarely online), ask to be added by a social media manager or marketing coordinator who checks Business Settings daily. Verify this person has **Admin** privileges on the Ad Account, not just the Page.

## Security Best Practices

Meta access controls are the first line of defense against ad account hackers.

1.  **Require 2FA:** Ensure your agency account and the client's account have Two-Factor Authentication enabled.
2.  **Principle of Least Privilege:** Only request "Ad Account Admin" (Finance access) if absolutely necessary. Standard "Advertiser" access is sufficient for 99% of campaign work.
3.  **Regular Audits:** Remove access for former employees immediately at the agency level.
4.  **Partial Access:** If managing only Instagram, you technically only need "Instagram Ad Account" access, though full Ad Account access is standard for agencies.
