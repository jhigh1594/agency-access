# WARP Framework Guide

The WARP Framework assesses four forces that **multiply together** (not add) to determine hypergrowth potential. A weakness in any one force dramatically reduces overall potential.

## The Four Forces

### P: Pervasive Pain (0-25 points)

**Definition:** Are you solving a problem the market is desperate to fix?

The best products solve problems that are:
1. **Widespread** - Affects millions, not thousands
2. **Frequent** - Users hit this daily/weekly, not monthly
3. **Loud** - People actively complain about it

#### Three Criteria

**Widespread (0-10 pts)**
- Could support $100M+ market?
- TAM (Total Addressable Market) size
- Not a niche problem for a tiny segment

**Frequent (0-10 pts)**
- Daily friction = daily switching opportunities
- Weekly is acceptable, monthly is too infrequent
- The more often users feel the pain, the more likely they'll switch

**Loud (0-5 pts)**
- 100+ strangers complaining online right now
- Visible pain, not silent suffering
- Search for "[problem] sucks" or "[current solution] frustrating"

#### How to Measure "Loud"

1. Search "[problem] sucks" or "[current solution] frustrating" - count results
2. Check Reddit threads - look for 50+ upvotes on pain-related posts
3. Twitter/X complaints - search complaints in last 30 days
4. Review sites (G2, Trustpilot) - negative reviews about this specific pain

**Scoring:**
- 10+ complaints = 1pt
- 50+ = 3pts
- 100+ active discussions = 5pts

#### Examples

| Product | Pain | Score | Why |
|---------|------|-------|-----|
| Cursor | Coding is tedious | 25/25 | Millions of devs, daily task, constant complaints |
| Midjourney | Visual creation constrained | 24/25 | Massive TAM, frequent need, loud demand |
| Linear | Issue tracking clunky | 22/25 | Large TAM, daily use, vocal dissatisfaction with Jira |

### W: Win Preference (0-25 points)

**Definition:** Do users try you once and can't go back?

**Formula:** Transformation (0-10) x Approach (0-10) x Defaults (0-5)

This isn't about being "better" - it's about making the old way feel obsolete.

#### Transformation (0-10)

Does your From→To statement make the old way feel obsolete?

**Test:** Would users say "I can't imagine going back"?

**Examples:**
- Cursor: "From typing every line → AI completes your thoughts"
- Gamma: "From spending hours in PowerPoint → Generate presentations in minutes"
- Notion: "From scattered docs in Word → Everything in one workspace"

#### Approach (0-10)

Does your workflow replace the old one, not just optimize it?

**Measures:**
- Time eliminated
- Expertise removed
- Steps collapsed
- Cost reduced

**Example:** Lovable eliminates months of dev work, not just makes it faster

#### Defaults (0-5)

Do you make decisions for users?

- What choices do you make vs. ask?
- 80% use case handled automatically
- Customization hidden by default
- Opinionated = sticky

#### Examples

| Product | Score | Why |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Cursor | 24/25 | Transforms coding experience, users can't go back to vanilla VS Code |
| Gamma | 23/25 | Transforms presentation creation, old way feels painful |
| Lovable | 22/25 | "20x faster" claim tested, but 70% solution not 100% |

---

### A: Activate Instantly (0-25 points)

**Definition:** Do users gain a new capability in 60 seconds or less?

This is about the "aha moment" - when users realize your product's value.

#### Time-to-Value Scoring

| Time to Value | Score |
|---------------|-------|
| Sub-60 seconds | 20-25 pts |
| 2-5 minutes | 15-19 pts |
| 5-15 minutes | 10-14 pts |
| 15+ minutes | 0-9 pts |

#### Three Types of Blockers

**Knowledge Gaps** - What must users understand?
- Agent solution: Makes decision for them

**Skill Gaps** - What must users execute?
- Agent solution: Does it for them

**Product Gaps** - What must users configure?
- Agent solution: Automates with defaults

#### Examples

| Product | TTV | Score | Why |
|---------|-----|-------|-----|
| Midjourney | 30 seconds | 24/25 | First image generated almost instantly |
| Cursor | Immediate | 25/25 | AI assistance starts as you type |
| Slack | 2-3 minutes | 18/25 | Need to create workspace, invite team |

#### Validation Tip

Your initial TTV estimate may be optimistic. If possible, watch 2-3 new users actually go through onboarding to validate timing. Founder estimates are often 2-3x faster than reality.

### R: Repeatable Leverage (0-25 points)

**Definition:** Can you scale revenue without scaling headcount?

This is the difference between a product business and a services business.

#### Three Criteria

**Zero Marginal Humans (0-10 pts)**
- 10,000th user costs same as 100th to serve
- No linear relationship between users and support staff

**AI + Product Does Work (0-10 pts)**
- Automation handles onboarding, support, conversion
- Product improves itself through usage

**Effortless ARR (0-5 pts)**
- Users upgrade themselves
- Revenue grows without sales team
- Self-serve pricing page

#### The 10,000 User Test

> "If 10,000 users signed up tomorrow, could we activate, convert, and keep them without adding headcount?"

If the answer is "no," you have a leverage problem.

#### Revenue per Employee Benchmark

| Company | ARR/Employee | Leverage |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Midjourney | $5M | Exceptional (40 employees, $200M ARR) |
| Cursor | ~$4M+ | Exceptional (small team, massive usage) |
| Typical SaaS | $200K-400K | Average |

#### Examples

| Product | Score | Why |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Midjourney | 24/25 | $200M ARR with 40 employees = $5M/employee |
| Cursor | 23/25 | Scales massively with minimal team |
| Linear | 20/25 | Strong PLG but some enterprise sales needed |

## Force Multiplication

The four forces multiply, not add. This means:

- 20 x 20 x 20 x 5 = 40,000 (one weak force kills you)
- 15 x 15 x 15 x 15 = 50,625 (balanced is better)

**Key insight:** A 60/100 with one 5/25 force is worse than 60/100 with four 15/25 forces.
