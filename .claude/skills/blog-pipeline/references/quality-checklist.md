# Quality Checklist

The 9/10 standard. This is the bar every post must clear before publication. Not every element is required for every type, but the more you hit, the higher the score.

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Publish-ready | Ready to ship. Original angle, sourced data, zero AI patterns. |
| 8-8.5 | Strong | Good content, needs tightening. One more editorial pass. |
| 7-7.5 | Good draft | Solid foundation, identifiable gaps. Targeted fixes needed. |
| Below 7 | Needs work | Structural issues or missing fundamentals. Re-evaluate approach. |

## What Separates 9/10 from 7/10

These are the differentiators observed in the actual 9/10 posts:

1. **Original angle** — No competitor covers this specific take. Not a rehash.
2. **3-5 sourced statistics** — Real attribution, not "studies show."
3. **Original framework** — A model, lifecycle, or decision tree the reader can apply.
4. **Honest positioning** — AuthHub is positioned accurately, not as a silver bullet. Includes "when NOT to use us" guidance.
5. **Strong internal linking** — 3-5 links woven naturally into the text, not dumped at the bottom.
6. **Zero AI-detectable patterns** — See banned words below.
7. **The reader thinks "this person understands my problem"** — Not "this person wants to sell me something."

## Banned Words & Phrases

These are instant quality killers. They signal AI-generated content to readers (and to Google's quality raters).

**Absolutely never:**
- delve into, delve
- leverage (use "use" or "take advantage of")
- robust
- cutting-edge
- empower
- transform
- seamless (use "smooth" or "simple" if needed, but prefer showing, not telling)
- synergy / synergies
- paradigm shift
- game-changing
- revolutionary
- unlock (as in "unlock potential")
- utilize (use "use")
- in today's fast-paced world
- it's important to note
- it's worth mentioning
- let's dive in / let's dive deeper
- at the end of the day
- in this article, we'll explore
- here's the thing
- the bottom line is
- a deep dive into

**Avoid (use sparingly, only if genuinely the best word):**
- comprehensive (show it, don't label it)
- innovative
- streamline
- optimize
- enhance
- tailored
- navigate
- landscape (as in "the digital landscape")
- ecosystem (unless literally talking about an ecosystem)

## Structural Quality Checks

### Opening
- [ ] Opens with the reader's problem, not the solution
- [ ] First paragraph contains the primary keyword naturally
- [ ] No generic filler ("In today's digital age...")
- [ ] Vignette, scenario, or specific statistic — not abstraction

### Body
- [ ] One idea per paragraph
- [ ] Short sentences (average 15-20 words)
- [ ] Active voice throughout (scan for "is/are/was/were + past participle")
- [ ] "You" appears more than "we" or "I"
- [ ] Specific numbers over vague claims
- [ ] No meta-commentary sections ("Why this matters", "The takeaway")
- [ ] Sections flow naturally — no label-style headers
- [ ] Every claim that could be questioned has support

### Closing
- [ ] Doesn't start with "In conclusion" or "To summarize"
- [ ] Ties back to the opening (bookend)
- [ ] Clear next step for the reader (even if it's just "apply this framework")
- [ ] AuthHub mention is natural, not forced

### Data & Sources
- [ ] Every statistic has a source or is reframed as observation
- [ ] External links go to authoritative sources (industry reports, platform docs, research)
- [ ] No unsourced "studies show" or "research indicates"
- [ ] Internal statistics (from your own data) are clearly attributed

## The 20-30% Cut Test

After completing the draft, read it and cut 20-30% of the words. Here's what usually goes:

1. **Redundant sentences** — If paragraph 2 says what paragraph 1 already said, one of them goes
2. **Qualifiers** — "if applicable", "where relevant", "in certain cases", "depending on the situation"
3. **Meta-commentary** — Sentences that explain why something matters instead of demonstrating it
4. **Hedge phrases** — "arguably", "in many ways", "to some extent", "generally speaking"
5. **Filler openings** — "It goes without saying that", "As you might expect", "Needless to say"
6. **Paragraphs that don't advance the argument** — If removing a paragraph doesn't change the reader's understanding, remove it

## Voice & Tone Checklist

AuthHub's brand voice from CLAUDE.md: **Bold, Efficient, Trustworthy**

- [ ] Direct and confident — knows the pain, has solved it
- [ ] Professional but not corporate — personality and expertise
- [ ] Action-oriented — focus on "done" not "doing"
- [ ] No salesy language — AuthHub is the answer to a problem, not a product pitch
- [ ] Conversational — writes like a respected peer, not a brand
- [ ] Specific language from brand guidelines:
  - Use: complete, unified, professional, predictable, transparent, simple, faster, streamlined, automated
  - Avoid: revolutionary, game-changing, cheaper
