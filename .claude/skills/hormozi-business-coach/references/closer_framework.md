# C.L.O.S.E.R. Sales Framework

## The Steps

### C — Clarify Why They're Here
Ask: "What made you reach out / show up / book this call?"
Let THEM tell you the problem. Don't assume. Don't pitch yet.

### L — Label the Problem
Reflect back what they said but with diagnostic precision.
"So what I'm hearing is you have [specific problem] that's costing you [specific pain]."
Make them feel understood before you prescribe anything.

### O — Overview Past Pain & Experience
Ask: "What have you tried before? What happened?"
This does two things: (1) surfaces objections early, (2) positions your solution against failed alternatives.
"So you tried X and Y, spent $Z, and you're still stuck. Does that sound right?"

### S — Sell the Vacation, Not the Plane Flight
Sell the DESTINATION (result), not the PROCESS (features).
- Wrong: "You'll get 12 modules, weekly calls, and a Slack community"
- Right: "In 90 days, you'll have a predictable system generating 10+ qualified leads per week without cold calling"
Paint the after state. Make it vivid. Make it specific.

### E — Explain Away Concerns
Don't handle objections — prevent them. Address the top 3 concerns before they voice them:
1. "Will this work for me?" → Proof/case studies
2. "Can I afford it?" → ROI framing ("This costs $X. It will generate $Y.")
3. "What if it doesn't work?" → Guarantee or risk reversal

### R — Reinforce the Decision
After they say yes, reinforce immediately:
"You just made the best decision you'll make all year. Here's exactly what happens next..."
Prevent buyer's remorse by creating excitement about the next step.

## Key Principles
- Never pitch before diagnosing
- The person who asks questions controls the conversation
- If you're talking more than 30% of the time, you're selling wrong
- Price is only an objection when value isn't clear
