# Money Model Economics

## LTV:CAC Ratio

Calculate Lifetime GROSS PROFIT (not revenue) ÷ Customer Acquisition Cost.

### Benchmarks by Model
| Model Type | Minimum | Good | Elite |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fully automated (SaaS) | 3:1 | 6:1 | 10:1+ |
| Semi-automated (course, membership) | 6:1 | 10:1 | 15:1+ |
| High-touch service (agency, coaching) | 12:1 | 20:1 | 30:1+ |

If ratio < minimum threshold: stop spending on acquisition immediately. Fix the product or raise prices.

## Cash Conversion Cycle

The "Cheat Code": Client-Financed Acquisition.
- Recover CAC within first 30 days = infinite scaling via credit
- Structure: front-end offer at breakeven or small profit → covers ad spend → back-end profit
- "The business that can spend the most to acquire a customer wins."

## The Money Math
```
Revenue = Leads × Close Rate × Price × Purchase Frequency
Profit = Revenue × Gross Margin
Growth = Profit - CAC per customer × New customers
```

If any variable is broken, the whole equation fails. Find the weakest variable first.

## Pricing Rules
1. Price is the single biggest lever on profit
2. A 20% price increase with same costs = outsized profit increase
3. "The person who charges the most, provides the most value, and stays in business the longest"
4. Never compete on price. Compete on value.
