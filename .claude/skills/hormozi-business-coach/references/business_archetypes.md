# Business Model Archetypes

Different business models have different constraint patterns and scaling paths. Match the archetype before prescribing.

## Local Service (Gym, Clinic, Salon, Agency-Local)
- **Key metric:** Lifetime Value per member/client
- **Default constraint:** Low volume (not enough leads)
- **Scaling path:** 6-week challenge offers → front-end liquidator → monthly recurring
- **Price lever:** Move from per-session to package/membership billing
- **Margin target:** 50-60% after labor
- **Trap:** Discounting to fill capacity. Never discount. Create a better offer instead.
- **Hormozi prescription:** "Your gym/salon/clinic is a local monopoly waiting to happen. Run a 6-week challenge at breakeven to fill the building, then convert to monthly EFT. The challenge IS your marketing."

## Agency / Done-For-You Service
- **Key metric:** Gross margin per client
- **Default constraint:** Owner in delivery (can't scale past 10-15 clients)
- **Scaling path:** Productize delivery → hire fulfillment → systematize onboarding
- **Price lever:** Value-based pricing tied to client outcomes, not hours
- **Margin target:** 80%+ or you're doing it wrong
- **Trap:** Scope creep. Custom work for every client. Productize or die.
- **Hormozi prescription:** "Stop customizing. Build one deliverable that works for 80% of clients. Fire the 20% who need custom work. Your margin will double overnight."

## Coaching / Info Product
- **Key metric:** Revenue per lead
- **Default constraint:** Can't scale past $3M without becoming a media company or software company
- **Scaling path:** 1-on-1 → group → course → community → licensing
- **Price lever:** Stack the offer (add bonuses, guarantee, scarcity)
- **Margin target:** 85%+ (almost all profit after content creation)
- **Trap:** Thinking content quality matters more than marketing. Wrong. Distribution > content.
- **Hormozi prescription:** "Your content is probably fine. Your offer sucks. Stack bonuses, add a guarantee, create real scarcity. Make it stupid not to buy."

## Software / SaaS
- **Key metric:** LTV:CAC ratio (minimum 3:1 for automated, 6:1+ if any human touch)
- **Default constraint:** Churn. Always churn.
- **Scaling path:** Reduce churn → increase ARPU → then add acquisition volume
- **Price lever:** Usage-based or tiered pricing. Annual contracts with discount.
- **Margin target:** 70-85% gross margin
- **Trap:** Spending on acquisition before fixing retention. Filling a leaky bucket.
- **Hormozi prescription:** "Stop buying ads. Fix your churn first. If people leave after 3 months, you don't have a growth problem, you have a product problem."

## E-Commerce / Physical Product
- **Key metric:** Cash conversion cycle (time from inventory purchase to cash in bank)
- **Default constraint:** Inventory = cash tied up = death if slow
- **Scaling path:** Reduce cash conversion cycle → upsell/cross-sell → subscription model
- **Price lever:** Bundling, subscriptions, limited editions
- **Margin target:** 60%+ after COGS (harder in e-com, which is why it's harder to scale)
- **Trap:** Inventory. Every dollar in inventory is a dollar not earning interest.
- **Hormozi prescription:** "Pre-sell before you buy inventory. Use cash conversion cycle compression — get paid before you pay suppliers. Subscription model if possible."
