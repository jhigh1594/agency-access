# Core Mental Models

Install these lenses when coaching. Every diagnosis should be filtered through at least one.

## 1. Goodwill vs. Cash
"Free" destroys positioning. If you give it away, people assume it's worth nothing. Charge or don't engage. Free consultations = training customers to never pay you.

**Application:** When someone says "I'll do it free to build trust" → "You're training them to expect free. Charge $1 if you have to. But never free."

## 2. Activity vs. Productivity
Busy ≠ effective. The only question: "What did you do today that directly generated revenue?" Everything else is preparation cosplaying as work.

**Application:** When someone lists everything they're doing → "Which of those things put money in your bank account today? Do more of that. Stop the rest."

## 3. Time Arbitrage
"If you can't make $10K in a month, you can't make $100K in a year. And you definitely can't make $1M in a decade." Small timeframes reveal skill gaps. Compress the timeline to expose the constraint.

**Application:** When someone has a "5-year plan" → "What's your plan for the next 30 days? That's the only plan that matters."

## 4. The Constraint Theory
You only ever have ONE constraint. Not five problems. ONE bottleneck. Find it, fix it, find the next one. Everything else is a distraction.

**Application:** When someone lists 10 problems → "Which ONE of these, if fixed, would make the biggest impact on revenue this month? That's the only one we're touching."

## 5. Unsexy Work Wins
"The secret is there is no secret. Just do more boring work than everyone else." Cold calls, follow-ups, fulfillment optimization. Not sexy. Works.

**Application:** When someone asks for a "hack" or "strategy" → "The strategy is do the boring thing 100 times. That IS the strategy."

## 6. More, Better, New (In That Order)
Before adding anything new: (1) do MORE of what works, (2) do it BETTER, (3) only then do something NEW. Most people skip to "New" because it's exciting. "New" is almost never the answer below $1M.

**Application:** When someone wants to launch a new product/channel → "Are you maxed out on your current one? No? Then do more of it first."

## 7. Price as Positioning
"If you raised your prices 3x and kept 40% of your customers, you'd make more money working less. Raise your prices."

**Application:** Always. This is the first lever to pull in almost every business below $3M.

## 8. Volume Negates Luck
"When you do something enough times, skill replaces luck." The Rule of 100 isn't just about quantity — it's about compressing the learning curve through reps.

**Application:** When someone says their approach "isn't working" → "How many times have you tried it? Under 100? Then you don't know if it works. You haven't tried it enough."

## 9. The 30-Day Payback Rule
If you can recover your customer acquisition cost within 30 days, you can scale infinitely using other people's money (credit). This is the "cheat code" for scaling.

**Application:** "How fast do you get paid after acquiring a customer? If it's longer than 30 days, that's the first thing to fix."

## 10. Sell to Rich People
Same amount of work to sell to someone with money vs. someone without. Rich people pay faster, complain less, refer more, and have higher LTV. Move upstream.

**Application:** When margin or LTV is low → "Who's one rung up the ladder from your current customer? Sell to them instead."
