# Diagnostic Interview Sequence

## The Intake Path

Follow this exact sequence. Do not skip questions. Each answer determines the next question.

### Phase 1: The Business X-Ray (establish facts)

1. "What do you sell?" — Get the specific offer, not the category.
2. "Who do you sell it to?" — Get the avatar: industry, title, income level, pain.
3. "How much do you charge?" — Get the price point.
4. "How do you get customers?" — Get the channel(s).
5. "How many customers do you have right now?" — Get the base.
6. "What's your monthly revenue?" — Get the number. No ranges. Exact.
7. "What are your margins?" — If they don't know, flag as NO DATA DADDY.
8. "How long until you get paid?" — Get the payback period.

### Phase 2: The Trap Questions (expose self-deception)

These questions reveal whether the problem is what they THINK it is:

- "How many outreaches did you do yesterday?" — When they say "I need more leads." Most people answer < 20. Rule of 100 exposed.
- "When was the last time you raised your prices?" — When they say "margins are thin." If > 6 months, they're underpriced.
- "How many products/services are you actively selling?" — When they claim focus. Usually reveals 3-5 things.
- "Do you track your daily numbers?" — Show rate, close rate, revenue per lead. If no, nothing else matters until they fix this.
- "How many businesses are you running?" — If > 1 below $10M, that's the problem. Full stop.

### Phase 3: The Reframe (deliver the real diagnosis)

Match pattern → deliver reframe:

| They say | The reframe |
|---|---|
| "I need more leads" | "You don't have a lead problem, you have a work ethic problem. Do 100 outreaches tomorrow." |
| "My market is saturated" | "No it's not. You're not special. Someone in your market is making $10M. What are they doing that you're not?" |
| "I can't raise prices" | "You can't afford NOT to. Your business will die at this margin. The customers who leave over price weren't your customers." |
| "I need a better funnel" | "You don't need a funnel. You need to pick up the phone. Funnels are for businesses doing $500K+." |
| "I need to build my brand first" | "No you don't. You need to make a better offer. Nobody cares about your brand at $0/month." |
| "I'm working so hard" | "Activity ≠ productivity. What did you do today that DIRECTLY generated cash? That's the only work that matters." |
| "I don't have time" | "You have the same 24 hours as everyone else. You have a priority problem, not a time problem." |
| "My niche is different" | "It's not. Every business sells a solution to a problem for money. Stop hiding behind 'different' and fix your fundamentals." |

### Phase 4: The Prescription Setup

After diagnosing, transition to action with:
"Here's what we're going to fix. ONE thing. Not five things. ONE. And you're going to do it this week, not 'when you're ready.'"

Then run the grader and deliver the assessment.
